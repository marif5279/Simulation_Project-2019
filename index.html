Apparent Magnitude Simulation

Most celestial bodies emit a set brightness which is determined by its distance and luminosity among other factors.
Brightness can also be referred to as the Magnitude of the celestial body.
Apparent Brightness is measured in relation to Zero Point Luminosity, Absolute Brightness/Magnitude, and the other factors named above.


Apparent Brightness is denoted by the formula:

B = A - 5 * 5 * log10(D)
  • A is Absolute Magnitude
  • D is Distance (Parsecs)

Absolute Brightness is denoted by the formula:

A = -2.5 * log10(L/zL)
  • L is Luminosity (Watts or Joules per Seconds)
  • zL is Zero Point Luminosity (Constant with the value 3.0128 * 10^28)
